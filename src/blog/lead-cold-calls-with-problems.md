<p>"What problem does your product or service solve?"</p>

<p>A question I ask to any sales team I work with.</p>

<p>Why?</p>

<p>Because that is what you should lead your cold call with.</p>

<p>Not case studies.</p>

<p>Not your customer base.</p>

<p>Not your features or benefits.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>Cold Calls Should Start With the Problem</h2>

<p>70% of B2B sales conversations start with a problem.</p>

<p>Negativity bias shows that negative experiences grab attention more than positive experiences.</p>

<p>This is not just sales jargon.</p>

<p>It is psychology.</p>

<p>Prospects are busy. They do not wake up hoping someone will call and explain a feature set to them.</p>

<p>They care about what is currently annoying them, slowing them down, costing them money, creating risk, or making their job harder.</p>

<p>If your opener does not connect to that, it is just noise.</p>

<h2>Most Salespeople Do Not Know What Problem They Solve</h2>

<p>Salespeople often have no idea what problems they solve and how those problems resonate in the prospect's world.</p>

<p>So we workshop it together.</p>

<p>Not in fluffy marketing language.</p>

<p>In words the prospect would actually use.</p>

<p>If your team says things like "we improve efficiency" or "we help businesses grow," that is not a problem. That is a brochure.</p>

<p>A real problem sounds specific.</p>

<p>"Your recruiters are wasting hours sorting through unqualified applicants."</p>

<p>"Your SDRs are booking meetings with people who will never buy."</p>

<p>"Your reps are discounting because their pipeline is empty."</p>

<p>Now we have something to talk about.</p>

<p>This is why <a href="/blog/cold-calling-business-acumen/">business acumen in cold calling</a> matters. You need to understand how the problem shows up in the prospect's business before you can talk about it properly.</p>

<h2>Ask Your Customers First</h2>

<p>The best people to ask are your customers.</p>

<p>Ask three questions:</p>

<ul>
    <li>What problem were you experiencing when you decided to work with us?</li>
    <li>How was it affecting you or the business at the time?</li>
    <li>Why do you continue to work with us?</li>
</ul>

<p>Do this with enough customers and you will start hearing the same problems again and again.</p>

<p>Pick the 2 to 3 most common ones.</p>

<p>You then have your cold calling pitch.</p>

<h2>It Really Is That Easy</h2>

<p>This is not about inventing a clever line.</p>

<p>It is not about pretending every prospect has the exact same problem.</p>

<p>It is about understanding the most common business problems your product or service fixes, then opening a conversation around them.</p>

<p>Once you lead with a problem, the call becomes about the prospect.</p>

<p>Lead with your product, and it becomes about you.</p>

<p>That is why most cold calls fail.</p>
