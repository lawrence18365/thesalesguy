<p>"The reason for a cold call is to introduce yourself, your company and then ask for the meeting."</p>

<p>I saw this on a recent post.</p>

<p>And it's exactly why cold calling has a bad rep.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>What That Advice Actually Sounds Like on a Call</h2>

<p>Here is what a call like that would sound like:</p>

<p><em>"Hi, I'm Lee, calling from ABC company, we specialise in helping your sales reps get more comfortable on the phone, leading to increased conversations and more meetings. Our clients average 30% extra rev after engaging us and we are trusted by some big brands like yours. Would you be interested to learn more?"</em></p>

<h2>What Your Prospect Actually Hears</h2>

<p>"Me, me, me, fake exaggerated case study number, made-up clients, straight for the appointment."</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "Calls like this aren't good. They're begging. They're needy. They have no care for the prospect."
</div>

<h2>Sales Calls Are Not About You</h2>

<p>Sales calls are not about "talking about yourself" and "booking meetings quickly."</p>

<p>They are about finding prospects you can genuinely help. Ones that have problems you can help with.</p>

<p>Not jamming everyone down your sales funnel.</p>

<p>The rep who rattles off their company pitch in the first ten seconds has already told the prospect everything they need to know — that this is going to be about the rep, not about them. The hang-up is almost guaranteed.</p>

<h2>Stop Pitching. Start Earning the Conversation.</h2>

<p>A good cold call does not open with a brag. It opens with a reason for the prospect to stay on the line. Ask for a small segment of their time. Offer them a way out. Lead with a problem you see in their world, not a feature you have in yours.</p>

<p>This is the same reason <a href="/blog/stop-begging-for-meetings/">begging for meetings</a> never works. Prospects can smell neediness through the phone. Fake stats and name-drops make it worse, not better.</p>

<p>Your job on the call is not to convince. It is to qualify. To find out whether there is a real problem, whether it is worth fixing, and whether you are the one to fix it. That's <a href="/blog/qualify-hard-disqualify-harder/">qualify hard, disqualify harder</a> — and it starts in the first thirty seconds.</p>

<h2>The Real Goal of a Cold Call</h2>

<p>Forget "introduce yourself, your company, and ask for the meeting."</p>

<p>The real reason for a cold call is to find out if there is a problem worth solving. Everything else — the intro, the company, the meeting — is downstream of that.</p>
