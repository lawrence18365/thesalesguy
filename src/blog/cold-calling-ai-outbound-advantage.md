<p>If you run a business and are looking at your outbound strategy, have a look at what your competition are doing.</p>

<p>I can guarantee all of them will be using email to reach prospects.</p>

<p>I can guarantee the same slop will also be copied and pasted into LinkedIn DMs.</p>

<p>Most of them are either looking at or have bought AI tools to push out the above at a much quicker rate but with "personalisation."</p>

<p>Thank you, Claude.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>AI Outbound Has Made Everyone Sound the Same Faster</h2>

<p>Email is not the problem.</p>

<p>LinkedIn is not the problem.</p>

<p>AI is not the problem.</p>

<p>The problem is everyone is using them to send the same message to the same prospects with the same fake relevance.</p>

<p>"Saw your recent growth."</p>

<p>"Noticed you're hiring."</p>

<p>"Thought this might be relevant."</p>

<p>Your prospect has seen it all before lunch.</p>

<p>AI has not made most outbound better. It has made average outbound quicker.</p>

<p>That is not a strategy. That is a louder version of the same problem.</p>

<h2>Only a Small Fraction of Your Competition Will Use the Phone</h2>

<p>Only a small fraction of your competition will be using the phone.</p>

<p>Not because it does not work.</p>

<p>Because it is uncomfortable.</p>

<p>You will face rejection.</p>

<p>It does not come naturally.</p>

<p>And to become good at it, you have to push past the pain barrier.</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "Because it's tough, that's exactly why it works. It's the easiest way to stand out."
</div>

<p>I speak to directors of companies every day.</p>

<p>Most tell me they have not received a cold call for weeks or months.</p>

<p>My first ever client told me they had not had a cold call for a year.</p>

<p>They were a listed company.</p>

<h2>The Phone Creates a Different Kind of Opportunity</h2>

<p>When everyone else hides behind email and LinkedIn, a good phone call stands out.</p>

<p>Not a scripted pitch.</p>

<p>Not a desperate meeting request.</p>

<p>A thoughtful business conversation.</p>

<p>One where you ask for permission, lead with a problem, listen properly, and work out whether there is anything worth discussing.</p>

<p>That is why <a href="/blog/cold-calling-vs-email-linkedin-qualified-meetings/">cold calling still wins on qualified meetings</a>. You can qualify in real time. You can hear tone. You can ask the next question. You can find out whether the prospect actually cares.</p>

<p>You cannot do that properly in a cold email sequence.</p>

<h2>Want to Work With Your Dream Clients?</h2>

<p>Pick up the phone.</p>

<p>It works.</p>

<p>Yes, it is uncomfortable.</p>

<p>That is the point.</p>

<p>Your competitors are choosing the comfortable option. They are automating more emails, sending more DMs, and pretending personalisation is the same as relevance.</p>

<p>You can choose the channel they are avoiding.</p>
