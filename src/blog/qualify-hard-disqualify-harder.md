<p>I was sitting in front of a CEO the other week. We were having a discovery conversation about his team.</p>

<p>I stopped him and asked:</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "Have you ever had a salesperson say no to you before?"
</div>

<p>"No," he replied.</p>

<p>"You have now."</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>Why I Walked Away From a Deal</h2>

<p>Sounds counter-intuitive, doesn't it?</p>

<p>I run a business. I sell cold calling training. Why would I walk away from a CEO who was willing to talk?</p>

<p>He had a small sales team, but when we dug into the numbers, the dials and conversations were already there. His reps were booking 3 meetings a day each. They had an 85% show rate. The deals were converting.</p>

<p>His product was new and highly reliant on massive immediate activity before an upcoming legislative change.</p>

<p>Could their actual cold calls improve? Yes. There is always room for refinement.</p>

<p>But interrupting their high-velocity activity at this exact moment to run them through a comprehensive <a href="/sdr-sales-training/">SDR Sales Training program</a> would have probably been a hindrance, not a help.</p>

<p>So, I decided not to pursue the deal.</p>

<h2>Stop Begging for the Sale</h2>

<p>Your job as a salesperson is not to convince, push, or <a href="/blog/stop-begging-for-meetings/">beg for the sale</a>.</p>

<p>Your job is to create an environment where the prospect feels comfortable enough to open up to you with the truth. You need to uncover their real problems.</p>

<p>If they cannot convince you that they urgently need your help, then there isn't a deal on the table.</p>

<p>This is a fundamental shift in mindset. We are so often taught to chase every lead and fill the pipeline with <a href="/blog/stop-chasing-vanity-metrics/">vanity metrics</a>. But clinging to deals that are not a perfect fit is the fastest way to waste your time and burn your credibility.</p>

<h2>The Power of Disqualification</h2>

<p>We teach this constantly in our <a href="/account-executive-training/">Account Executive Training</a>:</p>

<p><strong>Qualify hard. Disqualify harder.</strong></p>

<p>If you have the confidence to <a href="/blog/disqualify-early-turned-down-coffee/">disqualify early</a>, you earn the respect of your prospects. You stop acting like a desperate order taker and start acting like a trusted consultant.</p>

<p>Stop wasting time on prospects who don't need you right now. Go find the ones who do.</p>
