<p>"Use this opener."</p>

<p>"Just be yourself."</p>

<p>"Stop using pattern interrupts, they don't work."</p>

<p>"Use whatever feels comfortable."</p>

<p>"Ask for permission."</p>

<p>So much time is spent on this platform talking about cold call openers.</p>

<p>And whilst I have my opinion and agree some work better than others, it is such a small part of an outbound call.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>The Opener Has One Job</h2>

<p>Your opener has one purpose.</p>

<p>To get you into a conversation.</p>

<p>That is the easiest part of the call.</p>

<p>It is what comes next which is difficult.</p>

<p>Most reps do not fail because their first sentence was not perfect.</p>

<p>They fail because once the prospect says anything other than "yes, tell me more," they have no idea what to do.</p>

<p>They rush.</p>

<p>They pitch.</p>

<p>They ask weak questions.</p>

<p>They accept smokescreens.</p>

<p>Then they go back to LinkedIn looking for another magic opener.</p>

<h2>A Fancy Cold Call Opener Does Not Teach the Call</h2>

<p>A fancy new cold opener does not teach you:</p>

<ul>
    <li>How to pitch your services in a way that lands with your prospect</li>
    <li>How to handle smokescreen objections</li>
    <li>How to ask thought-provoking questions to get them to think</li>
    <li>Business acumen</li>
    <li>Active listening and understanding what you're listening for</li>
    <li>Pace and tone</li>
    <li>Different types of questions and when to use them</li>
</ul>

<p>That is the actual work.</p>

<p>The opener gets you through the door. It does not carry the conversation.</p>

<p>This is why I have written before about <a href="/blog/why-cold-call-openers-sound-like-begging/">why most cold call openers sound like begging</a>. A bad opener can ruin a call, but a good opener cannot save a salesperson who does not know how to run the conversation.</p>

<h2>Stop Looking for Fancy One-Liners</h2>

<p>Use whatever opener you want.</p>

<p>But if you get the rest wrong, you will always struggle.</p>

<p>If you cannot explain the problem you solve, the prospect will not care.</p>

<p>If you cannot hear a smokescreen objection, you will accept it as truth.</p>

<p>If you cannot ask a question that makes the prospect think, you will end up pitching.</p>

<p>If you do not understand their business, you will sound like every other rep interrupting their day.</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "Stop focusing on the fancy one-liners. Start focusing on how to handle a business conversation."
</div>

<h2>Business Conversations Lead to Qualified Opportunities</h2>

<p>That is what matters.</p>

<p>Not the opener that gets the most likes.</p>

<p>Not the pattern interrupt someone sold in a course.</p>

<p>Not the line that feels comfortable to say.</p>

<p>A cold call is a business conversation with a stranger who may or may not have a problem you fix.</p>

<p>Your job is to find out.</p>
