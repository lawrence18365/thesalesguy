<p>Too often I hear on cold calls:</p>

<p><em>"We help companies with X problem, how are you solving this currently?"</em></p>

<p>This is a very average way to start a cold call.</p>

<p>For two reasons.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>Do Not Tell the Prospect They Have a Problem</h2>

<p>Firstly, you're telling the prospect they have a problem.</p>

<p>You have no idea if they have the problem.</p>

<p>Assuming they do might get their back up.</p>

<p>It is their job to tell you if a problem exists.</p>

<p>Your job is to create the conditions for that to happen.</p>

<p>There is a difference.</p>

<p>If you open by telling them what is wrong, you sound like every other salesperson trying to make their pitch fit.</p>

<p>If you ask properly, they tell you what is wrong in their own words.</p>

<p>That is when the conversation becomes useful.</p>

<h2>Process Questions Lead to Logical Answers</h2>

<p>Secondly, the question only gets the prospect talking about their current process.</p>

<p>Process questions lead to logical answers.</p>

<p>But people do not buy with just logic.</p>

<p>They buy through their emotions.</p>

<p>Your job when cold calling is to see if the prospect has an emotional attachment to the problems you fix.</p>

<p>Emotions are things like fear, frustration, loss, annoyance, gain, and relief.</p>

<p>That is what tells you whether the problem matters.</p>

<p>A prospect can describe a process perfectly and still not care enough to change it.</p>

<p>That is why fact finding alone creates weak meetings.</p>

<h2>Logic Questions Move the Call Sideways</h2>

<p>Logic questions on their own will not land you meetings or get you sales.</p>

<p>They only move the conversation sideways.</p>

<p>"How are you doing it currently?"</p>

<p>"What system do you use?"</p>

<p>"How many people are involved?"</p>

<p>Fine questions in the right place.</p>

<p>But if that is all you ask, you end up with information, not motivation.</p>

<p>You need to drill down.</p>

<p>How does the issue affect the business?</p>

<p>How much time does it waste?</p>

<p>What happens if it stays the same?</p>

<p>Why has it not been fixed already?</p>

<p>How frustrated are they by it?</p>

<p>This is also why <a href="/blog/cold-call-training-5-minute-rule/">the 5 minute rule for cold calls is rubbish</a>. You cannot always find emotional attachment in a rushed, surface-level call.</p>

<h2>Start Looking for Emotional Attachment</h2>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "Stop trying to fact find. Start looking for emotional attachment."
</div>

<p>Once you hear emotional responses, you know you have a qualified meeting.</p>

<p>Because that is how people buy.</p>

<p>They justify with logic later.</p>

<p>But they move because the problem matters enough to them now.</p>
