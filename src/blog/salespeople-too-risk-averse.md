<p>The greatest things in life come from taking risks.</p>

<p>But not everyone has the same appetite for risk.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>A $5,000 Risk</h2>

<p>Twelve years ago, at the age of 22, I booked a one-way ticket to Australia from the UK.</p>

<p>I didn't know anyone here, had less than $5,000 to my name, no job, and just three days of accommodation booked. I had never even set foot in the country before.</p>

<p>At the time, I was met with questions from concerned family and friends:</p>

<ul>
    <li>"What if you don't find work?"</li>
    <li>"What if you don't like it?"</li>
    <li>"What if you miss home?"</li>
    <li>"Shouldn't you just find a grad scheme?"</li>
</ul>

<p>To them, the move was seen as a massive risk.</p>

<p>But for me? It was no risk at all. If I didn't like it here, I could just move back home. I could always earn the money back.</p>

<p>Twelve years later, and with a lot of hard work, I now call Australia my home. I couldn't be happier with the decision my 22-year-old self made.</p>

<h2>The Problem with Sales Reps Today</h2>

<p>So many people don't take enough 'risks' in life.</p>

<p>And that is because they think of everything that can go wrong. But they forget that almost everything is reversible.</p>

<p>Salespeople, in particular, are far too risk-averse.</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "What if they say no? What if they don't take sales calls? What if they're busy when I call?"
</div>

<p>This is the root cause of <a href="/blog/overcome-call-reluctance/">call reluctance</a>.</p>

<p>If you are sitting there today, dreading making calls, stop thinking about all the things that can go wrong. None of it is permanent. A bad call does not ruin your career. <a href="/blog/how-to-handle-rejection-in-sales/">Rejection is just part of the process</a>.</p>

<h2>Think About What Can Go Right</h2>

<p>Pick up the phone. Take the risk. Start believing in yourself, your product, and your service.</p>

<p>Instead of fearing the worst, start thinking of what can go right. You might just find the exact person who has been waiting for a solution like yours.</p>

<p>There is so much to gain, and very little to lose.</p>
