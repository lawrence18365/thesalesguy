<p>"Cold calling doesn't work in our industry."</p>

<p>A snippet of my cold calling career.</p>

<p>18 year old, worked in a bank. Cold calling worked.</p>

<p>The same year, worked as an estate agent. Cold calling worked.</p>

<p>21 year old, sold charity over the phone. Cold calling worked.</p>

<p>22 year old, moved to Australia. Sold life insurance. Cold calling worked.</p>

<p>26 year old, sold tech support into accounting practices. Cold calling worked.</p>

<p>29 years old, sold tax training into larger accounting practices. Cold calling worked.</p>

<p>30 years old, sold technical support to financial planning practices. Cold calling worked.</p>

<p>33 years old, launched my business. Cold calling worked.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>Cold Calling Works Across Industries</h2>

<p>I cold call in front of clients in multiple industries.</p>

<p>It works.</p>

<p>I get clients to cold call in front of me.</p>

<p>It works.</p>

<p>The industry excuse is one of the easiest ones to hide behind because it sounds sensible.</p>

<p>"Our buyers are different."</p>

<p>"Our market is relationship led."</p>

<p>"Our prospects don't pick up."</p>

<p>"Our service is too complex for cold calling."</p>

<p>I've heard all of it.</p>

<p>But every business has problems. Every buyer has pressures. Every director has things they would fix if someone helped them see the issue clearly enough.</p>

<p>The phone is just the channel.</p>

<p>The conversation is what matters.</p>

<h2>Maybe the Industry Is Not the Problem</h2>

<p>If your cold calls sound like a scripted pitch, of course they do not work.</p>

<p>If your reps open with features, rush to a meeting, and panic the second they hear a reflex objection, of course they struggle.</p>

<p>If they cannot explain what business problem they solve in the prospect's world, why would the prospect keep talking?</p>

<p>That is not an industry issue.</p>

<p>That is a skill issue.</p>

<p>Cold calling does not fail because your prospect works in finance, recruitment, tech, accounting, construction, insurance, or whatever other vertical you want to blame.</p>

<p>It fails because the call gives the prospect no reason to care.</p>

<p>That is why <a href="/blog/cold-calling-business-acumen/">business acumen matters in cold calling</a>. If you cannot understand what problems look like inside the prospect's business, you will always sound like someone reading a script.</p>

<h2>Bad Cold Calling Does Not Prove Cold Calling Is Dead</h2>

<p>Most people have never heard good cold calling.</p>

<p>They have heard reps pitching too early. They have heard people begging for meetings. They have heard fake pattern interrupts. They have heard salespeople trying to "touch base" with strangers who never asked them to.</p>

<p>Then they decide the whole channel is broken.</p>

<p>That is like watching someone miss ten penalties and declaring football does not work.</p>

<p>No. They are just bad at penalties.</p>

<p>Cold calling is a skill. Tone matters. Pace matters. Questions matter. Listening matters. Knowing when to push and when to leave it matters.</p>

<p>And yes, practice matters.</p>

<h2>The Phone Is Not the Problem</h2>

<p>If you honestly think cold calling does not work in your industry, ask a harder question.</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "Maybe it's not the phone that's the problem, but you."
</div>

<p>That might sound harsh.</p>

<p>It is also useful.</p>

<p>Because if the phone is the problem, there is nothing to fix.</p>

<p>If the call is the problem, you can fix it.</p>
