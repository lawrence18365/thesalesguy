<p>When cold calling, there are only two types of objections.</p>

<p>Reflex objections. And objections made up by the salesperson.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>1. Reflex Objections</h2>

<p>Reflex objections generally happen at the start of the call. It is when the prospect is in fight-or-flight mode, and uses an objection to get off the phone.</p>

<ul>
    <li>"Not interested."</li>
    <li>"I'm going into a meeting."</li>
    <li>"I'm busy."</li>
</ul>

<p>These can happen for a number of reasons, but mostly it comes down to the fact that the prospect does not feel comfortable with you just yet.</p>

<p><strong>Tip:</strong> Ask for a small segment of their time and offer them a way out. This immediately lowers their defenses and <a href="/blog/keep-control-cold-call/">keeps you in control of the cold call</a>.</p>

<h2>2. Objections Made Up By The Salesperson</h2>

<p>Any other objection is entirely made up by the salesperson.</p>

<p>Listen to these common phrases:</p>

<ul>
    <li>"We are using a competitor."</li>
    <li>"We have a preferred suppliers list."</li>
    <li>"We handle this internally."</li>
    <li>"This isn't a problem for us."</li>
    <li>"We don't have budget."</li>
</ul>

<p>None of these are objections. <strong>They are just statements.</strong></p>

<p>The prospect has not objected to anything. It does not mean everything is rosy and they are giving you a hard "no." It is an invitation for you to dig deeper and see if you can actually help.</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "Because salespeople see these as 'objections,' they believe they have to overcome them—which goes against human psychology."
</div>

<h2>Understand, Don't Convince</h2>

<p>As humans, we are much more likely to lean in when we feel understood, rather than when we feel like we are being convinced.</p>

<p>So, instead of arguing or trying to <a href="/blog/overcoming-objections-cold-calling/">"overcome" the objection</a>, ask a question and seek to understand.</p>

<p>For example: <em>"If there was one thing that competitor could be doing better, what would that look like?"</em></p>

<p>If you are in sales and struggling to 'overcome objections', this is a gentle reminder that those objections are mostly made up in your own head. View them as statements that need questioning. It will make your life a lot easier.</p>
