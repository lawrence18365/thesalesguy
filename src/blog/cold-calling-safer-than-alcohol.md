<p>Deaths caused by alcohol in Australia in 2014: 1,765.</p>

<p>Deaths in 2024 via cold call: 0.</p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>Happy Hour Yes, Power Hour No</h2>

<p>"Shall we grab a few at happy hour?"</p>

<p>Salespeople: "Sure!"</p>

<p>"Wanna make some dials for power hour?"</p>

<p>Salespeople: "No thanks."</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "Cold calling is a lot better for you than alcohol. And if the power hour doesn't go well, there is always beer."
</div>

<h2>The Fear Doesn't Match the Risk</h2>

<p>A stranger, who is probably having a bad day, might hang up on you. That is the worst case scenario on a cold call.</p>

<p>Let's run through what a bad call actually costs you:</p>

<ul>
    <li>No one close to you has died.</li>
    <li>You haven't died.</li>
    <li>No one close to you has gotten ill as a result.</li>
    <li>You haven't got ill.</li>
    <li>Your salary is still getting paid.</li>
    <li>So is your pension.</li>
    <li>You still have food in your fridge.</li>
    <li>You're not getting evicted as a result of the call.</li>
    <li>The call hasn't made you bankrupt.</li>
</ul>

<p>A few bad cold calls have zero effect on your life. <a href="/blog/how-to-handle-rejection-in-sales/">Rejection is not the problem</a> people make it out to be.</p>

<h2>Put the Reps In</h2>

<p>Most prospects respect the craft. Get onto the next one.</p>

<p>If you let a bad call drag you down, it will only ruin the next one. That is the actual risk — not the hang-up itself, but letting it sit in your head for the next 40 dials.</p>

<p>Power hour works the same way as the pub. You commit, you show up, you get through the round. The difference is one builds your career. The other builds a hangover.</p>
