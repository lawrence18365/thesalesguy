<p>140 dials.</p>

<p>30 conversations.</p>

<p>5 meetings.</p>

<p>Everyone in the comments:</p>

<p><em>"Well done. Those are great figures."</em></p>

<div class="section-divider"></div>

<h2>SDR Meeting Numbers Tell You Almost Nothing</h2>

<p>This is where we go wrong in the sales world.</p>

<p>How can you tell those are good numbers?</p>

<p>You can't.</p>

<p>Any salesperson can set up a meeting. Getting a meeting is not that hard.</p>

<p>Getting a qualified meeting is.</p>

<p>There is a massive difference between a prospect who agreed to a calendar invite because they wanted you off the phone, and a prospect who has a real problem, understands the impact, and wants to explore fixing it.</p>

<p>One is pipeline.</p>

<p>The other is a placeholder.</p>

<h2>The Questions That Matter Before You Celebrate a Meeting</h2>

<p>If you're setting meetings, and cannot answer the majority of the following, your meetings are nothing more than hit and hope:</p>

<ul>
    <li>Is the prospect the decision maker?</li>
    <li>What problem are they looking to fix and why?</li>
    <li>How long has the problem existed?</li>
    <li>What's the impact of the problem?</li>
    <li>What happens if the problem remains?</li>
    <li>What has the prospect tried to fix the problem?</li>
</ul>

<p>That is qualification.</p>

<p>Not "they were nice on the phone."</p>

<p>Not "they accepted the invite."</p>

<p>Not "they said they were happy to learn more."</p>

<p>I have written before about why <a href="/blog/how-to-run-sdr-team-dont-celebrate-meetings/">I don't celebrate meetings booked</a>. The point still stands. A meeting only matters if it has a reason to exist.</p>

<h2>Meetings Are Easy. Revenue Is Hard.</h2>

<p>Sales teams love simple numbers because they are easy to manage.</p>

<p>Dials. Conversations. Meetings.</p>

<p>They look clean on a dashboard. They make managers feel in control. They give SDRs something to post about on LinkedIn.</p>

<p>But none of them pay the bills on their own.</p>

<p>A calendar full of unqualified meetings just creates a new problem for the AE. They spend their week speaking to people with no authority, no pain, no urgency, and no intention of buying.</p>

<p>Then everyone wonders why close rates are low.</p>

<p>You do not have a closing problem. You have a qualification problem. I have covered that here: <a href="/blog/closing-problem-qualification-problem/">you don't have a closing problem, you have a qualification problem</a>.</p>

<h2>Ask the Only Number That Matters</h2>

<p>Next time you see one of those posts, don't ask:</p>

<p><em>"Can you get me 5 meetings too?"</em></p>

<p>Ask:</p>

<div class="article-pullquote">
    "How many of your 5 meetings turned into paying customers?"
</div>

<p>That is the question.</p>

<p>Meetings don't matter.</p>

<p>Revenue does.</p>
